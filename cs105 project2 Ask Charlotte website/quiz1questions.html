What are the traits of a healthy relationship?

A: Healthy. Give me a manipulative and controlling partner. I like that type of person.

B: Unhealthy. I don't want a manipulative and controlling partner. That's just putting myself through unnecessary pain.

C: Both??? Sometimes, I want my partner to be controlling and manipulative...I'm into that type of thing, is that bad?
A: You and your partner openly share your feelings and views honestly and with respect.

B: You and your partner repress your emotions, avoid the issue, and hope it will go away.

C: You and your partner duke it out by yelling and screaming at each other, not listening to a word either of you are saying, and break each others stuff.
A: No. Don't take care of yourself. It's better to neglect yourself and pour all of your energy into caring for your partner. Even if you're on the verge of passing out from pouring out all of your love, suck it up and keep neglecting yourself.

B: Yes. Taking care of yourself is healthy. That way, you can come back to the relationship energized and ready to give positivity and love to your partner. In addition, by taking care of yourself, you don't develop stress or resentment. "Me-time" is highly encouraged!

C: No. Just no. You should just not be in a relationship at all. They're torture.
A: Of course! Respect and valuing who you're with sets the foundation for a healthy, thriving relationship.

B: No. What does respecting and valuing your partner have to do with a good relationship? Personally, I've never respected my partners. I don't even see them as people.

C: I think A is the correct answer. Whoever thinks it's B has issues.
A: It depends. Sometimes, you end up with someone you're really compatible and similar to, so you end up agreeing on a lot of things. However, know that sometimes, disagreements will come up and that's okay. It's perfectly fine to disagree on some things.

B: Yes. You should agree with your partner on everything. Even if in the back of your mind you actually don't agree on something, it's better to lie and say that you do. That way, you can keep his or her love. Having differences can kill a relationship, you know?

C: I agree to disagree and not answer this question.
